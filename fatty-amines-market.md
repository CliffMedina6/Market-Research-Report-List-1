<p><p>Fatty amines are classified into three categories: primary, secondary, and tertiary fatty amines. Primary fatty amines are synthesized by the reaction between fatty acids and ammonia; they find application as emulsifiers, corrosion inhibitors, and surfactants. Secondary fatty amines are produced by reacting primary fatty amines with aldehydes or ketones. They are primarily used as additives in fabric softeners and antistatic agents. Tertiary fatty amines are formed by reacting secondary fatty amines with alkyl halides and are utilized in the production of quaternary ammonium compounds, which are widely employed as disinfectants and fabric conditioners.</p></p>

<p><p>Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy (DMD) is a genetic disorder characterized by progressive muscle degeneration and weakness. It primarily affects boys and is caused by a mutation in the dystrophin gene, resulting in the absence of dystrophin, a protein essential for muscle function. DMD Therapeutics refers to the various treatments and therapies aimed at managing the symptoms and slowing down the progression of the disease.</p><p>The current outlook for DMD Therapeutics is promising, with ongoing advancements in research and development. Currently, corticosteroids, such as prednisone and deflazacort, are the primary treatment options available for DMD. These drugs help to reduce inflammation and delay muscle degeneration, improving the quality of life for DMD patients.</p><p>However, there is a growing need for more effective and targeted therapies. Gene therapy, exon skipping therapies, and molecular therapies are emerging as potential therapeutics for DMD. These approaches aim to address the underlying genetic mutation and restore or replace the missing or defective dystrophin protein. Several clinical trials are underway to evaluate the safety and efficacy of these innovative therapies.</p><p>The future of DMD Therapeutics looks promising with the potential introduction of disease-modifying treatments. The introduction of gene therapies, such as gene editing or viral-based delivery systems, holds great promise in addressing the root cause of the disease.

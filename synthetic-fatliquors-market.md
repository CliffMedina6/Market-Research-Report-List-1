<p><p>The synthetic fatliquors market is segmented into three types: anionic, cationic, and non-ionic. Anionic fatliquors are negatively charged and are commonly used for soft, lightweight leathers. Cationic fatliquors have a positive charge and are suitable for leathers with a denser structure. Non-ionic fatliquors have no charge and are versatile, making them suitable for a wide range of leather types. These different types of fatliquors offer varying properties and benefits, allowing manufacturers to choose the most appropriate one based on the desired leather characteristics and end-use applications.</p></p>
